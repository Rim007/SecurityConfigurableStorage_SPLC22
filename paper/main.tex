\begin{abstract}
These day, software systems and their data storage are becoming increasingly individual, e.g., due to specific customer requirements.
Thus, the number of applications with diverse variants making both software systems and storage systems highly configurable is constantly growing.
However, their variability also significantly increases the relevance of data security as an important system property, for example to avoid the exploitation of potential vulnerabilities.
Unfortunately, there is a lack of a comprehensive overview of research regarding security of configurable systems and their data storage. 
To address this gaps, we conducted a systematic mapping study, focusing on 50 relevant publications of the last decade (2013--2022).  
The publications were analyzed regarding their proposed configurable systems, data storage, and security characteristics based on established classification criteria of the respective research fields.
Overall, security in the context of configurable systems and their storage seems underexplored and only to a small extent considered in a practice-oriented way, e.g., regarding relevant security standards. 
Furthermore, data storage and its security requirements are usually only mentioned briefly, even though they are either highly configurable or store data relevant to software product line-based software systems.
Our mapping study aims to help both practitioners and researchers to understand the current state-of-the-art research as well as its open issues and research directions.
\end{abstract}
